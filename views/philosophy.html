Philosophy

The philosophy of Confucius, also known as Confucianism, emphasizes moral cultivation and political ethics. He believed that through self-improvement and the establishment of social order, a harmonious society and individual happiness can be achieved. Confucius emphasized the importance of cultivating virtues such as benevolence, justice, and filial piety, as well as the practice of ritual and the pursuit of knowledge. His teachings have had a profound impact on East Asian culture and continue to influence modern Chinese society.
